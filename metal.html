Metal Roofing Information

Metal roofs are primarily made of steel or aluminum. Unlike aluminum panels, steel panels are coated with a metallic coating to prevent rust. Steel roofing is generally made into 24 to 26 guage thickness and aluminum thickness ranges from .023 to .040 thickness. Longevity is one of the main reasons customers choose metal roofs for their homes. If properly installed, a metal roof can last 2 to 3 times longer than asphalt shingles. Metal Roofs are also energy efficient, keeping your home cool in the summer and insulated in the winter. Dark asphalt shingles absorb radiation, heating the roof surface, where as Basic metal roofs reflect most of the radiation into the atmosphere, keeping the roof surface cooler.
