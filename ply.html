Single-Ply Roofing Information

Single-Ply roofing has become a popular choice in commercial roofing. There popularity is due to their easy installation, flexibility, and competitive pricing. Single-Ply roofs can be thermoset (compounded from rubber polymers) or thermostatic (bases on plastic polymers). These roof systems can be fully-adhered, loose-laid, mechanically-fastened, partially-adhered, protected membrane roof, self-adhering, or induction welded.

There are three major types of single-ply membranes:

TPO (thermoplastic polyolefin) - TPO is a thermoplastic membrane that is typically white. TPO is resistant to chemicals, grease, and fire. This membrane is good for roofs with high traffic.

PVC (poly-vinyl chloride) - PVC is a thermoplast membrane that is typically offered in gray, tan, or white colors. This membrane resists weathering, chemicals, and industrial pollutants.

EPDM (ethylene propylene diene monomer rubber) - EPDM, commonly refered to as "rubber roofing" is thermoset membrane that is usually black in color. This membrane is highly durable and is resistant to corrosive chemicals, diluted mineral acids and vegetable oils.
